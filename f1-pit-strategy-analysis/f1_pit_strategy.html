What the data says

KEY FINDINGS

01
One Stop Wins
On Bahrain's track layout, 1-stop strategies consistently delivered better finishing positions than 2-stop approaches — track position matters more than fresh rubber.
02
Soft Tyre Window
Soft compounds peak in the first 15–18 laps, then degrade sharply. Teams that miss the pit window lose ~1.5–2s per lap in the final stint.
03
Undercut Power
Drivers pitting 2–3 laps earlier than their rival gained on average 1.8 positions via the undercut — fresh tyre pace outweighs lost track position.
04
ML Confirms Strategy
Linear regression (R² ≈ 0.61) confirms average pace dominates results, but each additional pit stop statistically costs ~2.3 finishing positions.
